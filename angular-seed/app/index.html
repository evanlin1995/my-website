Evan Lin started playing the piano at the age of five under the tutelage of Joanna Zane-Fan. His accomplishments over the years include being a Rita Thompson scholarship recipient, an MTNA national finalist, the youngest winner of both the high school and the collegiate divisions of the Aloha International Piano Competition, and a performer on NPR's "From the Top." In April of 2011, Evan was named semifinalist and prizewinner of the John Bayless award (for the best performance of the commissioned work) in the Virginia Waring International Piano Competition. As a result, he gave the world premiere of Bayless’s "Winter Tableau: A Ballade for Left Hand” in the McCallum Theatre, Palm Desert. In the summer of 2012, Evan was a winner of the Aloha International Piano Concerto Competition, and also took second prize in the 2012 International Institute for Young Musicians (IIYM) Piano Competition. The following summer, Evan competed once more in the IIYM competition and took first prize. In addition to competing, Evan has performed as a soloist with the Honolulu Symphony, Hawaii Youth Symphony 1, Kamuela Philharmonic Orchestra, and Punahou Symphony Orchestra. Some of the concerti he played included Beethoven’s Piano Concerto No. 2, Rachmaninoff’s Piano Concerto No. 2, and Prokofiev’s Piano Concerto No. 3. Aside from playing the piano, Evan was a part of his school's orchestra as a first violinist and the jazz band as an electric guitarist. He has also participated in the Punahou Chamber Music Program as both a violinist and a pianist. Evan currently attends Stanford University and studies with Dr. Frederick Weldy, senior lecturer of piano.
